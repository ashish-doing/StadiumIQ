The Problem

Match day runs on guesswork

Forty thousand fans move through a handful of gates in a language mix spanning three host countries. Staff improvise. Bottlenecks form before anyone notices.

✕ Without StadiumIQ

  • ✕Fans ask staff who don't speak their language
  • ✕Crowd bottlenecks are noticed after they form
  • ✕Volunteers improvise protocols from memory
  • ✕Sustainability numbers are a post-event guess

✓ With StadiumIQ

  • ✓Navigation grounded in the actual venue map, auto-translated
  • ✓Gemini flags zones crossing 80% capacity in real time
  • ✓Protocol answers grounded in the actual knowledge base
  • ✓Per-match carbon estimate from the real transport mix

Feature Guide

Four tools, one operations layer

Fan Navigator

Ask for gates, restrooms, transit, or seating in plain language. Gemini detects the language automatically and answers grounded in the actual venue map — it won't invent a gate that doesn't exist.

"How do I get to my seat from the metro station in Hindi" → responds in Hindi, cites real gates

Crowd Intelligence Dashboard

A live-look zone density simulator feeds Gemini real capacity numbers every few seconds. When a zone crosses 80%, Gemini drafts specific, actionable redirect alerts — not generic safety copy.

"Zone A at 91%, rising" → "Deploy staff to Zone A, redirect incoming fans toward Gate D"

Volunteer / Staff Assistant

Staff query real operational scenarios — medical emergencies, lost children, security incidents. Gemini answers strictly from the matched knowledge-base entry, and says so honestly when nothing matches.

"Medical emergency in Section 114?" → grounded_on: ["Medical Emergency Protocol"]

Sustainability Tracker

Enter attendance, transport-mode split, and average distance — get a real per-match CO₂ estimate and Gemini suggestions targeted at the actual dominant transport mode, not generic advice.

40,000 fans · 60% car → suggestions specifically address the car share
